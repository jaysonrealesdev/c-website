Academic Policies

Academic Year

The academic year is divided into two (2) semesters with at least eight- een (18) weeks each, and a summer term of not less than six (6) weeks. The first semester usually opens in June, the second semester in November and the sum- mer session in April. The opening and closing of classes normally follow the Commission on Higher Education's (CHED), approved calendar of activities for every school year. All academic units of the school operate under the semestral system in which classes are scheduled from Monday through Saturday including computer laboratory classes.

Academic Load

The regular academic load for undergraduate students will depend on the curriculum of the program/course he is enrolled. However, a graduating student with satisfactory academic records may be permitted to carry an addi- tional load of not more than six (6) units. Subjects with pre-requisites and co- requisites shall be sequentially observed.

Classification of Students

Students are classified as regular, irregular, short-term course and spe- cial students. Students are classified as regular, irregular, short-term course and special students.

  • A regular student - is one who follows an organized pro- gram of study for bachelor's degree or undergraduate diploma/certificate. He is registered for normal academic load. He carries the subjects called for in the curriculum for which he is registered in a given semester.
  • An irregular student - is one who is registered for academic load but does not carry the full semestral load called for, by the curriculum for which he is registered in a given semester.
  • A special student - is one who is enrolled in another college or university but is cross-enrolled at a given semester.
Freshman (Incoming 1st Year)

The basic requirement for eligibility for admission in CAT College Legazpi, Polangui and Ligao Campuses educational programs shall be a graduate from a secondary level program recognized by the Department of Education.

Other Requirements:

Upon enrollment, the student should present the following credentials:

A. For High School Graduates:

  • Report Card or Form 138
  • Certificate of Good Moral Character
  • Two (2) pcs. Ix1 I.D. Picture
  • Birth Certificate issued by the Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA)

B. For Transferees:

  • Certificate of Transfer Credential
  • Certified True Copy of Official Transcript of Record for evaluation purposes.
  • Certificate of Good Moral Character
  • Cat College Entrance Examination.
  • Birth Certificate issued by the Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA).

Senior High School graduates who enrolled in college but the strand/ track is not in line with the course they take, will have to undergo enhancement programs, because they did not go through the SHS specialized subjects leading to the college course.

Entrance Examination

Entrance examinations are given daily in April, May and October of each year. The examination is scheduled 8:30 a.m. and 1:30 p.m. from Mondays through Fridays and Saturdays from 8:00-12:00 a.m. Upon release of the examination results, eligible applicant shall report for a personal interview with the respective Department head.

National Service Training Program

The National Service Training Program (NSTP) Law or Republic Act 9163 is a program aimed at enhancing civic consciousness and defense prepared- ness in the youth by developing the ethics of service and patriotism while under- going training in any of its three (3) program components specifically designed to enhance the youth's active contribution to the general welfare.

Program Components of the National Service Training Program

  • Reserve Officers' Training Corps (ROTC) which is designed to provide military training to tertiary level stu- dents in order to motivate, train, organize and mobilize them for national defense preparedness.
  • Literacy Training Service (LTS) which is designed to train the students to teach literacy and numeracy skills to school children, out-of-school youth and other segments of society in need of their services.
  • Civic Welfare Training Service (CWTS) which refers to activities contributory to the general welfare and the betterment of life for the members of the community of the enhancement of its facilities, especially those developed to improving health, education, environment, entrepreneur- ship, safety recreation and moral of the citizenry and other social welfare services.
Enrolment

All incoming freshmen students, male and female enrolled in any bac- calaureate degree courses are required to complete one (1) NSTP component of their choice as a graduation requirement. Each of the NSTP compo- nents shall be undertaken for an academic period of two (2) semesters and credited for three (3) units per semester with 54 to 90 training hours per semester. The NSTP was first implemented last School Year (SY) 2002 - 2003.

Old/Returning Students

Renewal of registration of a student is dependent on his previous performance. Refusal of re-enrollment/readmission to old/returning student shall be applied for violation of the rules and regulations of the school. An old student shall be readmitted upon presentation of his clearance duly signed by the school officials concerned.

Registration

Qualified applicants for admission should register within the period of registration. Registration includes filling out the prescribed form and paying the required fees. Students enrolled during the preceding semester/ summer are re-enrolled upon presentation of their clearance duly signed by concerned school officials. A student must be officially registered in order to receive credit for course work.

Cross Registration

Students registered in other institutions and who wish to cross reg- ister in CAT College must present a permit to cross enroll from the Dean or Registrar. The permit should state in writing the description of subjects and the total number of units, of which the student is allowed and the subjects that he is authorized to take. CAT College/AIT stu- dents maybe allowed to cross-enroll to other institutions provided that they seek permission from the Registrar and upon accomplishment of a prescribed form of the subjects that he is authorized to take.

On-the-Job Training

Part of the curricular offerings of the institutions is the On-the-Job Training (OJT) offered on the last summer or the last term of a particular course curriculum which actualize the students theoretical know-how. This will bridge the gap between theory and practice. The number of hours required is dependent on the course. The student shall submit an evaluation and a certificate of OJT at the end of the OJT period within 25 days after graduation to the corresponding Department Head duly signed and graded by the Head or Supervisor, Office or School where the students have completed their OJT. The OJT is both an aca- demic and a graduation requirement.

Changing/Adding or Dropping of Subjects

Changing/Adding/Dropping of subjects for valid reasons should be made through an application form for approval by the respective Department Head and Registrar of the school. This should be made within one (1) week after the close of the registration. The total load allowed however, shall not ex- ceed the maximum under the rules on academic load, or that which is provided for his curriculum year during the term/semester. A subject or subjects added to one's academic load unofficially or with- out prior approval by the Department Head/Registrar shall not be given credit.
